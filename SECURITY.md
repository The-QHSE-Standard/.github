# Security Policy

The QHSE Standard publishes content through public repositories and a static
library site. We take the integrity and availability of that content seriously.

## Reporting a vulnerability

**Please do not open a public issue for security problems.**

Instead, report privately using
[GitHub's private vulnerability reporting](https://github.com/the-qthe-standard/.github/security/advisories/new).
If that is unavailable, contact us via
[LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/company/the-qhse-standard).

When reporting, please include:

- A description of the issue and its potential impact
- Steps to reproduce (URLs, payloads, or configuration)
- Any relevant logs or screenshots

## What to report

Examples of issues we want to hear about:

- Vulnerabilities in our published site or build pipeline (e.g. injection, XSS)
- Exposed secrets or credentials in any repository
- Compromised dependencies or supply-chain concerns
- Defacement or unauthorized modification of published content

## Our commitment

- We will acknowledge a valid report as soon as we are able.
- We will keep you informed as we investigate and remediate.
- We will credit reporters who wish to be acknowledged, once a fix is released.

Thank you for helping keep our readers safe.
