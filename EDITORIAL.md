# Editorial Standard & Transparency

The QHSE Standard is a **Synthetic Media Lab** — **AI-Native, Human-Curated**.
We believe that being open about *how* we produce our work is itself a safety
principle: in the same way Safety-II asks us to understand work-as-done, we
think readers deserve to understand publishing-as-done. This page is that
disclosure.

## What we do

We translate established safety science — Safety-II, Resilience Engineering,
Human Factors, and Human & Organizational Performance (HOP) — into practical,
visual insight for managers, engineers, and leaders. We communicate **recognized
paradigms**. We do **not** invent bespoke operational safety advice.

## How a piece is made

1. **Sourcing.** Every article starts from recognized literature or primary
   incident reports. Claims are traced to citable sources.
2. **AI-assisted drafting.** We use AI to draft, structure, summarize, and
   generate visuals at scale. This is the "Synthetic Media" part — and we are
   explicit about it.
3. **Human curation.** A human reviews every published piece for:
   - **Faithfulness** — that frameworks and original authors are represented
     accurately and not distorted.
   - **Accuracy** — that claims match their sources.
   - **Clarity** — that it serves practitioners, not just specialists.
4. **Correction.** Published work is not frozen. When an error is found, we fix
   it in the open (see below).

## Our principles

- **Sourced, not asserted.** If we can't cite it, we don't claim it.
- **Faithful representation.** We stand on the shoulders of named authors and
  frameworks; we credit them and try not to misrepresent them.
- **Transparency over polish.** We disclose our use of AI rather than hide it.
- **Scope honesty.** We communicate paradigms for learning and discussion — not
  jurisdiction-specific safety advice. For that, consult a qualified
  professional.

## Corrections

Found a factual error, misattribution, or broken claim? Please
[open a correction issue](https://github.com/the-qthe-standard/.github/issues/new/choose).
We track and resolve corrections publicly — accuracy matters more than ego.

## A note on limitations

AI-assisted production can introduce subtle errors, even with human review. That
is precisely why the correction channel above exists and why we source our
claims. If something looks wrong, it might be — tell us.
